The 80/20 Rule for Loksewa Preparation

Focus on the most important 20% of the syllabus that will give you 80% of the results. Identify high-yield topics by analyzing previous year question papers and prioritize your study accordingly.

This approach ensures that you cover the most frequently asked and important topics first, giving you a solid foundation before diving into more specialized areas.

Expert Advice

Focus on Understanding, Not Memorization

Many candidates make the mistake of trying to memorize everything without understanding the underlying concepts. This approach might work for some factual questions, but it fails when you encounter application-based or analytical questions.

Instead, focus on understanding the core concepts and principles. When you truly understand something, you can apply it to different scenarios and questions, even if they're framed in unfamiliar ways. This approach also makes your learning more durable and reduces the need for constant revision.

Rajesh Sharma

Former Section Officer, PSC Member

Practice Makes Perfect: The Power of Mock Tests

Taking regular mock tests is one of the most effective preparation strategies. They help you familiarize yourself with the exam pattern, improve your time management, identify your strengths and weaknesses, and reduce exam anxiety.

I recommend taking at least one full-length mock test every week in the months leading up to your examination. After each test, thoroughly analyze your performance to understand where you went wrong and what you need to improve. This feedback loop is crucial for continuous improvement.

Sita Adhikari

Loksewa Preparation Expert

Stay Updated with Current Affairs

Current affairs constitute a significant portion of the Loksewa examination, especially for officer-level positions. Develop a habit of reading newspapers and following reliable news sources daily. Focus on national and international events, government policies, appointments, awards, and socio-economic developments.

Create a system to organize and revise current affairs regularly. I recommend maintaining a dedicated notebook or digital document where you can jot down important events and developments, categorized by themes like politics, economy, environment, etc. Review this regularly to ensure you don't forget important information.

Binod Thapa

Current Affairs Specialist
