Examination Overview

The Kharidar examination is conducted by the Public Service Commission of Nepal for recruitment to the Non-Gazetted Second Class positions in the civil service. The examination consists of two written papers followed by an interview. Each paper carries 100 marks and is of 2 hours duration.

Examination Pattern

Stage Paper Subject Full Marks Pass Marks Time
Written Examination Paper I General Knowledge & Current Affairs 100 40 2 Hours
Paper II Office Practice 100 40 2 Hours
Interview Personal Interview 20 - -
Total 220

Question Pattern

The question pattern for each paper typically includes:

  • Multiple Choice Questions (50 marks)
  • Short Answer Questions (30 marks)
  • Long Answer Questions (20 marks)

Eligibility Criteria

  • Nepali Citizenship
  • SLC/SEE or equivalent qualification from a recognized institution
  • Age: Minimum 18 years and maximum 35 years (relaxation as per rules for reserved categories)
  • Must have cleared the preliminary examination (if applicable)

Paper I: General Knowledge & Current Affairs

2 Hours 100 Marks

This paper tests the candidate's knowledge of general awareness, current affairs, and basic understanding of various subjects relevant to Nepal and the world.

1. Nepal's Geography and Natural Resources

  • Geographical features of Nepal: mountains, hills, valleys, and plains
  • Major rivers, lakes, and watersheds
  • Climate and ecological regions
  • Natural resources: forests, minerals, water resources
  • Environmental issues and conservation

2. Nepal's History and Culture

  • Major historical events and personalities
  • Unification of Nepal
  • Cultural heritage, festivals, and traditions
  • Ethnic groups, languages, and religions
  • Art, architecture, and literature

3. Nepal's Political System

  • Constitutional development in Nepal
  • Key features of the Constitution of Nepal
  • Structure of government: federal, provincial, and local
  • Political parties and electoral system
  • Fundamental rights and duties

4. Nepal's Economy and Development

  • Economic indicators and development status
  • Agriculture, industry, and service sectors
  • Development planning and policies
  • Poverty, unemployment, and economic challenges
  • Foreign aid, trade, and economic cooperation

5. General Science and Technology

  • Basic scientific concepts and principles
  • Major scientific discoveries and inventions
  • Information and communication technology
  • Health, nutrition, and diseases
  • Science and technology in daily life

6. Current Affairs

  • Major national and international events
  • Recent appointments, awards, and honors
  • Sports, literature, and cultural events
  • Recent government policies and programs
  • Contemporary socio-economic and political issues

7. Basic Mathematics and Logical Reasoning

  • Basic arithmetic operations
  • Percentage, ratio, and proportion
  • Simple interest and time-work problems
  • Logical reasoning and problem-solving
  • Data interpretation: tables, charts, and graphs

Paper II: Office Practice

2 Hours 100 Marks

This paper focuses on the basic principles and practices of office management, record keeping, correspondence, and administrative procedures relevant to government offices in Nepal.

1. Office Management Basics

  • Concept and importance of office management
  • Functions and responsibilities of an office
  • Office layout and environment
  • Office equipment and supplies
  • Office procedures and routines

2. Record Management

  • Importance of record management
  • Types of records and their classification
  • Filing systems and procedures
  • Record retention and disposal
  • Basic indexing and cataloging

3. Official Correspondence

  • Types of official letters and their formats
  • Drafting official letters, memos, and notices
  • Language and style in official correspondence
  • Handling incoming and outgoing mail
  • Email and digital communication

4. Meeting Management

  • Types of meetings and their purposes
  • Preparing for meetings
  • Agenda preparation
  • Minute writing and follow-up
  • Meeting protocols and procedures

5. Basic Financial Management

  • Introduction to government accounting
  • Budget and expenditure management
  • Basic financial rules and regulations
  • Procurement and inventory management
  • Cash handling and petty cash management

6. Personnel Administration

  • Attendance and leave management
  • Personnel records and files
  • Basic service rules and regulations
  • Office discipline and code of conduct
  • Staff welfare and motivation

7. Customer Service

  • Principles of good customer service
  • Handling inquiries and complaints
  • Communication skills
  • Public relations in government offices
  • Citizen charter and service delivery

8. Basic Computer Applications

  • Computer fundamentals
  • Word processing and document preparation
  • Spreadsheets for basic calculations
  • Email and internet usage
  • Digital record keeping

Preparation Tips

The following tips and strategies will help you prepare effectively for the Kharidar examination and maximize your chances of success.

Study Plan and Time Management

Create a Realistic Schedule

Develop a study schedule that allocates time for both papers. Give more time to your weaker areas while maintaining regular revision of stronger subjects.

Divide and Conquer

Break down the syllabus into smaller topics and set daily/weekly targets. Complete one topic before moving to the next, ensuring thorough understanding.

Regular Revision

Allocate specific time for revision of previously studied topics. Use spaced repetition techniques to improve retention and recall.

Current Affairs Update

Set aside 30 minutes daily for current affairs. Read newspapers, follow news websites, and maintain a current affairs diary.

Subject-wise Preparation Strategy

General Knowledge

Focus on Nepal-centric topics like history, geography, and governance. Create concise notes for quick revision. Practice with previous year questions to understand the pattern.

Office Practice

Understand the concepts thoroughly rather than memorizing. Relate theoretical knowledge to practical applications in government offices. Practice writing answers for procedural questions.

Mathematics and Reasoning

Practice regularly with different types of problems. Learn shortcuts and quick calculation methods. Focus on accuracy rather than speed initially.

Computer Knowledge

Learn basic computer terminology and applications. Understand the practical aspects of using computers in office settings. Practice basic operations if you have access to a computer.

Practice and Mock Tests

Previous Year Papers

Solve previous year question papers to understand the pattern, type of questions, and expected answers. Analyze the trends and frequently asked topics.

Mock Tests

Take regular mock tests to simulate exam conditions. This helps in building stamina, improving time management, and identifying areas for improvement.

Group Study

Form or join a study group to discuss difficult topics, share resources, and practice together. Teaching others is one of the best ways to reinforce your own understanding.

Self-Assessment

Regularly assess your preparation level. Identify your strengths and weaknesses, and adjust your study plan accordingly. Focus more on areas where you need improvement.

Exam Day Strategy

Time Management

Allocate time for each section based on marks. Start with questions you are most confident about. Leave some time for revision at the end.

Read Instructions Carefully

Read all instructions and questions carefully before starting to write. Understand what is being asked and answer accordingly.

Multiple Choice Strategy

For MCQs, eliminate obviously wrong options first. If unsure, make an educated guess rather than leaving it blank. There is no negative marking in most Loksewa exams.

Stay Calm and Focused

Maintain your composure during the exam. If you find a difficult question, move on and come back to it later. Focus on maximizing your score.
