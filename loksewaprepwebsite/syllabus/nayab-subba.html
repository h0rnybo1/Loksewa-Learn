Examination Overview

The Nayab Subba examination is conducted by the Public Service Commission of Nepal for recruitment to the Non-Gazetted First Class positions in the civil service. The examination consists of three written papers followed by an interview. Each paper carries 100 marks and is of 2.5 hours duration.

Examination Pattern

Stage Paper Subject Full Marks Pass Marks Time
Written Examination Paper I General Knowledge & Current Affairs 100 40 2.5 Hours
Paper II Office Management 100 40 2.5 Hours
Paper III Language Proficiency (Nepali & English) 100 40 2.5 Hours
Interview Personal Interview 20 - -
Total 320

Question Pattern

The question pattern for each paper typically includes:

  • Long Answer Questions (15-20 marks each)
  • Short Answer Questions (8-10 marks each)
  • Very Short Answer Questions (4-5 marks each)
  • Objective Questions (Multiple Choice, True/False, Fill in the blanks)

Eligibility Criteria

  • Nepali Citizenship
  • Intermediate (10+2) or equivalent qualification from a recognized institution
  • Age: Minimum 18 years and maximum 35 years (relaxation as per rules for reserved categories)
  • Must have cleared the preliminary examination (if applicable)

Paper I: General Knowledge & Current Affairs

2.5 Hours 100 Marks

This paper tests the candidate's knowledge of general awareness, current affairs, and basic understanding of various subjects relevant to Nepal and the world.

1. History and Geography of Nepal

  • Major historical events and personalities of Nepal
  • Unification of Nepal and its historical significance
  • Physical, political, and economic geography of Nepal
  • Natural resources, climate, and ecological regions
  • Major rivers, mountains, and geographical features

2. Nepalese Society and Culture

  • Social structure, cultural diversity, and traditions
  • Major festivals, arts, and cultural heritage
  • Ethnic groups, languages, and religions of Nepal
  • Social issues and challenges in contemporary Nepal
  • Cultural preservation and promotion

3. Governance and Political System

  • Constitutional development in Nepal
  • Key features of the Constitution of Nepal
  • Structure of government: federal, provincial, and local
  • Civil service and public administration
  • Political parties and electoral system

4. Economy and Development

  • Economic indicators and development status of Nepal
  • Agriculture, industry, and service sectors
  • Development planning and policies
  • Poverty, unemployment, and economic challenges
  • Foreign aid, trade, and economic cooperation

5. Science and Technology

  • Basic scientific concepts and principles
  • Major scientific discoveries and inventions
  • Information and communication technology
  • Environmental science and conservation
  • Science and technology development in Nepal

6. Current Affairs

  • Major national and international events
  • Recent appointments, awards, and honors
  • Sports, literature, and cultural events
  • Recent government policies and programs
  • Contemporary socio-economic and political issues

7. General Mathematics and Logical Reasoning

  • Basic arithmetic operations and calculations
  • Percentage, ratio, and proportion
  • Simple interest and compound interest
  • Logical reasoning and problem-solving
  • Data interpretation and analysis

Paper II: Office Management

2.5 Hours 100 Marks

This paper focuses on the principles and practices of office management, record keeping, correspondence, and administrative procedures relevant to government offices in Nepal.

1. Introduction to Office Management

  • Concept, importance, and objectives of office management
  • Functions and responsibilities of an office
  • Office layout and environment
  • Office equipment and technology
  • Modern office management techniques

2. Record Management

  • Importance and principles of record management
  • Types of records and their classification
  • Filing systems and procedures
  • Record retention, preservation, and disposal
  • Electronic record management

3. Correspondence and Communication

  • Types of official correspondence
  • Drafting official letters, memos, and notices
  • Communication channels and methods
  • Effective communication skills
  • Handling incoming and outgoing mail

4. Meeting Management

  • Types of meetings and their purposes
  • Planning and organizing meetings
  • Agenda preparation and circulation
  • Minute writing and follow-up actions
  • Meeting protocols and procedures

5. Financial Management

  • Basic concepts of government accounting
  • Budget preparation and implementation
  • Financial rules and regulations
  • Procurement procedures and inventory management
  • Financial reporting and auditing

6. Personnel Administration

  • Recruitment, selection, and placement
  • Training and development
  • Performance evaluation and promotion
  • Leave rules and attendance management
  • Staff welfare and motivation

7. Office Procedures and Practices

  • Standard operating procedures
  • Work flow management
  • Time management techniques
  • Customer service and public relations
  • Office security and confidentiality

8. Information Technology in Office

  • Computer applications in office management
  • Word processing, spreadsheets, and presentations
  • Database management and information systems
  • Internet, email, and digital communication
  • E-governance and digital Nepal initiatives

Paper III: Language Proficiency

2.5 Hours 100 Marks

This paper tests the candidate's proficiency in Nepali and English languages, focusing on grammar, vocabulary, comprehension, and writing skills essential for official communication.

1. Nepali Language (50 Marks)

  • Grammar and Usage:
    • Parts of speech (नाम, सर्वनाम, विशेषण, क्रिया, etc.)
    • Tenses and their applications
    • Sentence structure and transformation
    • Active and passive voice
    • Direct and indirect speech
  • Vocabulary and Word Formation:
    • Synonyms and antonyms
    • Word formation and derivatives
    • Idioms and phrases
    • Technical and administrative terminology
    • Common errors in Nepali usage
  • Comprehension and Writing:
    • Reading comprehension
    • Summarizing and précis writing
    • Essay writing on contemporary topics
    • Official letter writing and notice drafting
    • Report writing and documentation

2. English Language (50 Marks)

  • Grammar and Usage:
    • Parts of speech (noun, pronoun, verb, adjective, etc.)
    • Tenses and their applications
    • Articles, prepositions, and conjunctions
    • Active and passive voice
    • Direct and indirect speech
  • Vocabulary and Word Usage:
    • Synonyms and antonyms
    • Word formation and derivatives
    • Idioms and phrases
    • Technical and administrative terminology
    • Common errors in English usage
  • Comprehension and Writing:
    • Reading comprehension
    • Summarizing and précis writing
    • Essay writing on contemporary topics
    • Official letter writing and notice drafting
    • Report writing and documentation

Preparation Tips

The following tips and strategies will help you prepare effectively for the Nayab Subba examination and maximize your chances of success.

Study Plan and Time Management

Create a Balanced Study Schedule

Develop a study schedule that allocates time for all three papers. Give more time to your weaker areas while maintaining regular revision of stronger subjects.

Topic-wise Preparation

Break down the syllabus into smaller topics and set daily/weekly targets. Complete one topic before moving to the next, ensuring thorough understanding.

Regular Revision

Allocate specific time for revision of previously studied topics. Use spaced repetition techniques to improve retention and recall.

Current Affairs Update

Set aside 30-60 minutes daily for current affairs. Read newspapers, follow news websites, and maintain a current affairs diary.

Subject-wise Preparation Strategy

General Knowledge

Focus on Nepal-centric topics like history, geography, and governance. Create concise notes for quick revision. Practice with previous year questions to understand the pattern.

Office Management

Understand the concepts thoroughly rather than memorizing. Relate theoretical knowledge to practical applications in government offices. Practice writing answers for procedural questions.

Language Proficiency

Strengthen your grammar fundamentals in both Nepali and English. Practice writing essays, letters, and reports regularly. Read quality literature to improve vocabulary and comprehension.

Mathematics and Reasoning

Practice regularly with different types of problems. Learn shortcuts and quick calculation methods. Focus on accuracy rather than speed initially.

Answer Writing Practice

Regular Practice

Practice writing answers under timed conditions regularly. This improves your writing speed, organization, and time management during the actual exam.

Structured Answers

Develop a clear structure for your answers with introduction, main body, and conclusion. Use headings, subheadings, and bullet points to organize your thoughts logically.

Previous Year Papers

Solve previous year question papers to understand the pattern, type of questions, and expected answers. Analyze the trends and frequently asked topics.

Mock Tests

Take regular mock tests to simulate exam conditions. This helps in building stamina, improving time management, and identifying areas for improvement.

Exam Day Strategy

Time Management

Allocate time for each question based on its marks. Start with questions you are most confident about. Leave some time for revision at the end.

Read Instructions Carefully

Read all instructions and questions carefully before starting to write. Understand what is being asked and answer accordingly.

Presentation Matters

Write neatly and legibly. Use proper formatting with headings, paragraphs, and bullet points. Highlight key points to make your answer stand out.

Stay Calm and Focused

Maintain your composure during the exam. If you find a difficult question, move on and come back to it later. Focus on maximizing your score.
